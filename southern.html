Yala National Park

Missing Yala National Park while Sri Lanka sightseeing might bring regret to wildlife lovers. It is home to a host of wildlife and birds you are bound to run into a group of elephants bathing in streams, tossing their trunks wildly or leopards nestling lazily on tree branches. You could opt for the safari drives or a nature trail among the thick green foliage of the forest. Some tourists also camp at Yala and enjoy a barbeque under the stars. With so much to see and do here, Yala is indeed one of the best tourist places in Sri Lanka. Yala is the perfect place to try your hand at wildlife photography. Getting to Yala National Park is quite simple. All you will have to do is drive down to the Park from Colombo. Another way is by taking a bus to Tissamaharama.

Mirissa Whale Watching Tour

Experience the thrill of whale watching on this morning boat tour from Mirissa. Witness these incredible creatures off the southern coast of Sri Lanka. See a variety of animals including blue whales, sperm whales and dolphins from the observation deck.

Galle Fort

Galle is an important city in Sri Lanka and is a one-stop destination wherein you can take in all sights and sounds. One of the best places to visit in Sri Lanka is the Galle Fort. The Galle Fort area has wide cobblestoned roads and a number of eateries and cafes. Try and catch the sunset while you are there. There are a number of trinket shops and galleries that you can visit. It is among the most visited places in Sri Lanka. Another picturesque destination for sightseeing in Sri Lanka is the Japanese Peace Pagoda. Quite close to the Unawatuna beach, it is a white dome of serenity.

Bundala National Park

Full Day Game drive to Bundala National Park is a 7-8 hours game viewing opportunity with MICE Team. This is an important wintering ground for migratory water birds. You will be picked up from your hotel in Thissamaharama or Yala at 05:00 am and then moved to Bundala National Park. 06:00 - Start your game drive in Bundala which is an important wintering ground for migratory water birds. Bundala harbors 197 species of birds, the highlight being the greater Flamingo, which migrate in Large Flocks. Migrants and vagrants make the journey from as far as Siberia over 10,000 shorebirds might be feeding at any one time between October and March. 12:00 Enjoy your Picnic lunch in a nice location at the National Park. 13:30 Proceed with the second round of your safari to spot some more animals. Occasional Elephant, Spotted Deer, Sambar, Hanuman Langur, Toque Monkey, Water Monitor and Marsh Crocodile can be seeing during your Safari. 16:30 Leave the national park and proceed to your hotel.
